The Holistic Understanding

So often, the people who come to me are grappling with more than one thing at once, and finding that each is making the others harder to bear. Anxiety that is also compromising sleep; stress that has concretised into chronic physical tension; perimenopause symptoms that are making everything else harder to manage: these things compound each other, and this can be hard on the body. Reiki works with the whole system rather than a single symptom, so my clients often experience improvements in a variety of areas, from reduced anxiety to improved sleep, and a relaxation of tension in the whole body.

Reiki for Stress and Burnout

Stress is the most common reason people come to me. Many of us spend years powering through stress before we notice the toll it has taken on our bodies and minds. The result can be burnout: where our energy reserves for even ordinary tasks are exhausted and our usual resilience to meet life each day is worn away. Reiki can help restore this energy, and many clients find that daily life feels more manageable and the weight of stress and burnout begins to lift.

Burnout brings a particular kind of tiredness, the sort ordinary rest no longer seems to ease. A weekend away might help for a day or two, and then the familiar depletion returns. When the body has been running at full pelt for months without sufficient downtime, the effects begin to spread. Motivation fades, focus becomes harder to hold, and even small interactions can feel like an effort. Work you once found meaningful can start to feel drudgerous, and many people only see what is happening once the tank is well and truly empty.

Moreover, chronic stress and sustained pressure gradually narrow what therapists call the window of tolerance — the bandwidth within which we can meet demands and process emotions. The smaller it gets, the less it takes to tip you into overwhelm.

Recovery depends on genuine rest, the kind busy and capable people rarely allow themselves. A Reiki session gives you an hour with no demands and nothing to prove, and for someone deeply depleted, that hour of true rest can matter more than it sounds. By helping the body shift out of a prolonged stress response and into deep rest, Reiki can ease physical tension and gradually rebuild a sense of steadiness and capacity. It works alongside any medical care you are receiving, and it makes no claim to lighten your workload or treat a medical condition. Nevertheless, clients often leave feeling clearer in their thinking, calmer in their body, and more themselves again after a long period of depletion.

If you recognise yourself in any of this, whether you would call it burnout or simply a sense of running on empty, you would be very welcome here.

Reiki & Perimenopause: Support During a Significant Transition

Perimenopause is the years-long hormonal transition leading up to menopause, and for many women it proves far more disruptive than many people understand. It can bring disrupted sleep, low energy, brain fog, mood swings, joint stiffness, and a sense of alienation from yourself. These symptoms can be misunderstood by those close to women suffering their effects. In addition to the holistic benefits to body and mind, my Reiki sessions provide a judgement-free environment where clients are given the time and space to be understood and to be themselves.

The symptoms are largely invisible to those on the outside, and many women find that even those close to them do not fully understand what they are going through. A Reiki session is one of the few places where nothing is asked of you. For an hour, you do not have to explain yourself, manage how you come across, or hold anything together.

What is less often discussed is why perimenopause leaves so many women feeling depleted in ways that rest alone cannot fix. In her Sunday Times bestselling Preparing for the Perimenopause and Menopause, Dr Louise Newson, one of the UK's leading menopause specialists, explains how oestrogen plays a significant role in regulating sleep quality and the body's capacity to recover. As levels fall and fluctuate unpredictably, sleep becomes less restorative even when it is unbroken, and the body's ability to replenish itself between demands is genuinely reduced. The tiredness perimenopause brings often does not resolve with better habits or more rest. It is a shift in the body's baseline, driven by hormonal change rather than circumstance.

Reiki cannot alter your hormone levels. What it can do is support the body into a state of deep rest, and in doing so, help restore some of the equilibrium that perimenopause tends to erode. It works alongside your GP, your HRT, or any other care you have in place.

What clients often notice after a session:

  • Easier, deeper sleep
  • A reduction in physical tension held in the body
  • More steadiness from one day to the next
  • A clearer sense of feeling like themselves again
  • The experience of being genuinely supported through a demanding stage of life

Reiki, Anxiety & Low Mood

I know from experience that anxiety rarely shows up as a single symptom. It can manifest in myriad ways: racing thoughts and restlessness, nausea, palpitations, dizziness or lightheadedness, breathlessness, even the fear of fainting; for many, women especially, it's intensely physical. And whether it's generalised anxiety, health anxiety, a panic disorder, or anxiety that's yet to be named, it can feel relentless and take a real toll on daily life.

I came to this work in part through my own search for relief from severe anxiety and depression. I know how distressing and exhausting they can be, and it is largely why I feel called to serve others navigating their anxiety and low mood.

If you are struggling, you are far from alone. Reiki sits alongside the care your GP or therapist provides, and offers a regular hour of rest and steady support, without any need for referral or waiting lists.

Just one session of real rest can offer a genuine reset. My clients often notice their physical tension easing, their thoughts quietening during the session, and a calm that lingers into the hours and days afterwards. Those encumbered by low mood frequently describe feeling lighter, more like themselves, and more able to meet the day.

Above all, it's an opportunity to rest, reset and remember what calm feels like. If any of this feels familiar and you are unsure whether Reiki is right for you, you are welcome to get in touch before booking.

What Is Reiki, and How Can It Help You?

Physical tension, emotional stress, and energetic imbalance are deeply connected. Reiki works with all three.

A Modern Stress-Reduction Therapy with Ancient Roots

Reiki is a Japanese energy healing practice developed by Mikao Usui in the early 20th century. The name combines two Japanese concepts: "Rei" (universal) and "Ki" (life force energy), representing the fundamental energy that flows through all living beings. In practice, a session involves light touch or hands held just above the body, working with the body's own capacity to rest, rebalance, and recover.

Reiki is now offered within NHS hospital trusts including University College London Hospitals and Guy's and St Thomas', and is acknowledged by Cancer Research UK and Macmillan Cancer Support as a supportive complementary therapy used in UK cancer centres and hospices.

The Founder of Reiki: Dr. Mikao Usui

Dr. Mikao Usui (1865-1926)

Born in Japan in 1865, Dr. Mikao Usui was a lifelong student of medicine, psychology, and spiritual practice. In March 1922, after 21 days of fasting and meditation on Mount Kurama, he experienced a profound awakening and received the understanding of how to channel universal energy — what we now know as Reiki. Throughout his lifetime, he went on to train over 2,000 students before his passing in 1926, initiating a lineage of teachers that eventually brought Reiki to the West.

Today, I am honoured to carry on this tradition through my own practice, connected to Dr. Usui through an unbroken lineage of dedicated teachers.

How Reiki Works

During a session, I work gently across specific points of the body, hands placed lightly on or just above the surface, guiding universal life energy to where it is needed most. In the Usui tradition this is known as reiji-ho, and involves allowing the energy itself to guide the placement of the hands, rather than relying on a fixed sequence or conscious direction. The process supports the body's natural relaxation response and helps restore a sense of balance and ease.

Reiki is intelligent and highly adaptable: it can be administered through in-person sessions or distance healing, making it accessible regardless of location. The practice is entirely non-invasive, safe for people of all ages, and can even benefit animals.

Authentic Lineage

Reiki is passed from teacher to student in an unbroken line of transmission dating back to Dr Mikao Usui. This lineage honours the integrity of the practice and ensures the teachings remain authentic and true to their origins.

I received my Reiki training through Julie Hayes, an FHT member, whose lineage traces directly back to Dr Usui through William L. Rand, Hawayo Takata, and Dr Chujiro Hayashi. The full transmission is shown below.

William L. Rand studied with several Reiki Masters from different lineages: Diane McCumber and Marlene Schilke in 1989, Cherie Prasuhn in 1990, and Leah Smith in 1991. Studying with several teachers to deepen one's training was common during this period of Reiki's development in the West.
